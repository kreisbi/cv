What is my Research about?

  • Redispatch Markets in Germany ( Abschlussbericht)

    • In this project we looked at how a market-based incentives in the redispatch-framework would impact the redispatch in Germany. Our quantitative modelling showed that market-based
    incentives could indeed trigger stratigic bidding (Gaming) that in turn would increase redispatch by a significant amount, rather than reducing it.

  • Defining und Monitoring of the Security of Supply at European Power Markets ( 1. Projektbericht)

    • Here we looked at the European power markets while focussing Germany, and assessed the level of the so-called "Loss of Load Probability" (LoLP) for future years, up until 2030.
    We concluded that even with a high share of energy beeing supplied by renewable energy assets, the LoLP remains well below cirical tresholds, which means that demand can be fullfilled
    at nearly any given point in time.

  • Impact of Bidding Zone Splits on Redispatch in CORE-Region

    • We concluded that Bidding Zone Splits will not change the amount of congestion in the transmission grid, and therefore redispatch remain on a similar level as without them.
    The reason for this beeing that the requirements on "Minimum Remainig Margins", which stem from the Clean Energy Package, are limiting the relieving effects of a Bidding Zone Split.
